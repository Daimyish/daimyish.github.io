Tempus the Archmage

2D Platformer 2023 Unity, C#

The Problem

I wanted to build a 2D platformer that went beyond simple run-and-jump mechanics. The goal was a game where level architecture itself told a story — where room transitions, hidden paths, and enemy placement all communicated narrative without a single line of dialogue text.

My Role

Sole Game Designer. I handled level design, enemy AI scripting, difficulty pacing, and environmental storytelling across the full project.

Process

  • Designed multi-room levels with deliberate difficulty ramps: early rooms teach a mechanic in isolation, mid-rooms combine mechanics, and late rooms test mastery under pressure. Each room was blockout-tested before art pass.
  • Created hidden areas that reward exploration — secret rooms gated behind non-obvious wall jumps or timing puzzles, giving completionists something to find without punishing casual players who miss them.
  • Scripted enemy AI behavior trees in C# — enemies have patrol states, aggro detection radii, attack patterns, and retreat logic, creating encounters that feel reactive rather than scripted.
  • Used environmental storytelling through tileset changes, lighting shifts, and background object placement to imply narrative progression (a crumbling tower, a frozen library, an active forge) without cutscenes.

Outcome

The finished prototype demonstrated that level design and AI scripting could carry narrative weight in a genre that often relies on text boxes. Playtesters consistently identified the intended story beats from environment alone.

Reflection

This was my first project where I treated level design as narrative design. The biggest lesson was restraint — early drafts had too many hidden areas, which diluted the reward of finding any one of them. Cutting half the secrets made the remaining ones feel genuinely special.
