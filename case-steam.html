STEAM Dreams

3D Adventure/RPG Summer 2024 Universal Phoenix Group

The Problem

STEAM Dreams is an education-focused 3D adventure/RPG spanning K-12 STEAM categories, shipping to Steam through Universal Phoenix Group and Block By Block Publishing. The game needed engaging mini-game level layouts that could teach science, technology, engineering, art, and math concepts through quests, puzzles, and NPC interactions — without feeling like homework.

My Role

Game Design Intern focused on Level Design and QA. I designed and iterated on mini-game level layouts, performed systematic quality assurance passes, and contributed gameplay-flow improvements to the broader quest structure.

Process

  • Designed level layouts for mini-games within the larger RPG world, iterating on player pathing, difficulty pacing, and visual clarity so educational objectives were embedded in gameplay rather than overlaid on top of it.
  • Performed systematic QA passes across multiple game builds, documenting bugs with detailed reproduction steps, expected vs. actual behavior, and priority ratings to help the team triage effectively.
  • Contributed improvements to gameplay flow — specifically around quest gating, NPC dialogue pacing, and how puzzles signaled their objectives to players of different age groups.

Outcome

STEAM Dreams is listed on Steam and moving toward release. The QA documentation I produced helped the team close over a dozen blocking bugs before the next milestone build, and the level layout iterations improved early-playtest completion rates for the mini-games I worked on.

Reflection

Working on an education game forced me to think about accessibility in ways combat-focused games don't — a 7-year-old and a 14-year-old need different signaling for the same puzzle mechanic. The internship also taught me how professional QA workflows operate at scale, which informed how I approach testing in my own projects.
