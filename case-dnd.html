D&D Campaign Authorship

Tabletop Narrative Design 2018–Present 4-6 Players

The Problem

Running a compelling D&D campaign for years means designing a living system — not a script. Players derail plans, ignore plot hooks, and find solutions you never imagined. The challenge is building a world robust enough to handle improvisation while maintaining coherent story arcs and mechanically balanced encounters.

My Role

Dungeon Master and campaign author. I design the world, write the quests, build the encounters, voice the NPCs, and adapt everything in real time at the table.

Process

  • Authored multi-session campaigns with original lore — custom pantheons, political factions, geographic history, and cultural systems that give players a world to inhabit rather than a track to follow.
  • Designed branching quest lines where player decisions have persistent consequences. A village they saved in session 3 sends reinforcements in session 12. A faction they betrayed sends assassins. Every choice writes future content.
  • Built encounter tables balanced across party level, action economy, and terrain — using spreadsheets to calculate expected damage output, HP thresholds, and turn economy before bringing encounters to the table.
  • Created NPCs with distinct motivations, speech patterns, and mechanical roles. Recurring characters track player reputation and adjust their behavior across sessions based on prior interactions.

Outcome

Seven years of continuous campaigns with the same core player group. Players cite the persistent world consequences and NPC depth as why they keep coming back — the world feels like it remembers them.

Reflection

D&D is the purest systems design lab I have. Every campaign session is a live playtest with immediate feedback. It trained me to think in systems rather than scripts — to build rules and relationships that generate emergent narrative rather than trying to author every possible outcome. That mindset directly shaped how I approach encounter design, card balance, and player agency in digital games.
