About

I'm Matt — a systems and gameplay designer based in Falls Church, Virginia. I graduated from George Mason University in 2025 with a BFA in Computer Game Design and a CompTIA Tech+ certification.

I think about games as systems of interlocking rules that create emergent experiences. Whether I'm balancing a deck of 30 cards, scripting enemy AI behavior trees, or designing a node-map that makes every roguelike run feel different, my focus is always on the invisible architecture that makes gameplay feel alive.

Outside of game dev, I've been running D&D campaigns for seven years — which is basically live systems design with immediate playtesting feedback. I've also built 5+ custom gaming PCs, composed original music for projects, attended GAMESCOM 2018 in Cologne, and worked as an EYES Fellow at the Smithsonian Museum of Natural History.

Before pivoting fully into game design, I spent years as a swim instructor and lifeguard — work that taught me adaptive communication, real-time problem solving, and staying calm when things go sideways. Those skills translate directly to team-based development and playtesting under pressure.
